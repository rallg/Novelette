Templates

Novelette provides templates for several popular US and UK book sizes. The processed PDF is acceptable by nearly all P.O.D. Services.

Novelette cannot use word processor templates (such as those designed for MS Word). It cannot export your document to a word processor format.

Some P.O.D. Services provide MS Word templates, for the convenience of writers who use a word processor. That is not a problem, as long as you can ignore the MS Word template, and submit a finished PDF file.

If you employ an Editor who requests an MS Word document for editing, that may or may not be a problem. If the edited text is returned to you for final changes, you can export the Word document to UTF-8 plain text (no BOM) then markup the text according to Novelette requirements. The risk is that you will fumble the markup! But if you are using an Editor who accepts PDF, and returns an annotated PDF to you, then you can use Novelette directly.

If your P.O.D. Service does not accept PDF, consider using another Service. It seems to be that case some P.O.D. Services are really in the business of E-books, with print-to-paper as a nuisance sideline.

Available Trim Sizes

Novelette understands only a few sizes, which are among the most popular industry standards offered by P.O.D. Services.

Note that the small Pocket and A-Format sizes are not available. This is because P.O.D. often requires clearances that are unusually large for these formats, leaving an undesirably small printable area. Besides, the modern equivalent is a smartphone with E-book reader!

Size names "Novella" and "Memoir" are nicknames used by some Services; no particular content is implied. The other names are industry standards.

Sizes are listed in order of increasing width. Note that some services list sizes with height shown first. Height always exceeds width.

P.O.D. Services usually require clearance (margin) of at least 0.5in or 13mm on all sides of the printable area, with an additional 0.125in or 3mm at the inside (spine) edge, where pages are glued. These values are built into the templates. You do not need to set them. However, you may visually balance top/bottom clearances, using the optional upshift setting.

You do not set font point size, or lines per page. These are automatically set by the template.

Available Templates

\template{group number}{named size}

Templates are grouped as number 1, 2, 3. Each group number is compatible with several named sizes. Y9ou must choose both a number and a named size. Most named sizes are available in more than one group number.

Within a group, you may change the named size, without affecting line breaks or page numbering. So if you are not sure whether your book will be printed as Novella or B-Format, choose group 1. But if you are not sure whether your books will be printed as Digest or B-Format, choose group 2.

Note: A name change should always work, but possibly may result in different line breaks and pagination. If it does, minor edits may fix it.
